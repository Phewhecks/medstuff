Common Medical Emergencies and Extreme Symptoms

In case of any medical emergency, please consult a healthcare provider immediately or visit the nearest hospital. Below are some extreme symptoms and actions that need urgent medical attention:

  • Chest Pain or Pressure - Symptoms of heart attack (seek emergency care immediately).
  • Severe Shortness of Breath - Could be a sign of a serious respiratory issue (urgent attention required).
  • Uncontrollable Bleeding - Seek immediate treatment to prevent shock or other complications.
  • Severe Headache and Vomiting - Could indicate a neurological emergency like a stroke (seek medical help right away).
  • Loss of Consciousness - If someone loses consciousness or is disoriented, call for emergency help.
  • Severe Burns or Chemical Exposure - Immediate medical care is necessary to minimize damage.
  • Extreme Allergic Reactions (Anaphylaxis) - Requires urgent treatment with an epinephrine shot and emergency care.

What to Do in Case of an Emergency

If you or someone you know is experiencing a medical emergency, follow these steps:

  • Stay Calm: Panic will only make the situation worse. Take a few deep breaths and try to stay calm.
  • Call for Help: Dial 102 or the local emergency number to request an ambulance or immediate medical help.
  • Administer First Aid: If you're trained, administer basic first aid while waiting for emergency responders to arrive.
  • Be Clear and Specific: When calling for help, explain the situation clearly and provide accurate details about symptoms and the location.
  • Follow Instructions: Follow the instructions provided by medical professionals, even if you're waiting for them to arrive.
