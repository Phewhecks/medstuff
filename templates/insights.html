Diabetes Insights

Age Distribution (Diabetes)

This chart shows the distribution of people with diabetes across different age groups. It helps us understand if diabetes is more common at certain ages. Typically, diabetes is more common in older populations, particularly after the age of 45. However, with the rise of lifestyle-related factors, type 2 diabetes is increasingly being diagnosed in younger individuals as well.

BMI vs Glucose Levels (Diabetes)

This graph shows the relationship between Body Mass Index (BMI) and glucose levels in people with diabetes. It helps us see if higher BMI is linked to higher glucose levels. Key Insight: A BMI above 30 is considered obese, and people in this category are more likely to have elevated glucose levels or even develop type 2 diabetes. An average BMI range falls between 18.5 to 24.9, which is considered healthy. Levels above this range indicate increasing risks for health issues, including diabetes.

Blood Pressure by Diabetes Outcome

This chart shows how blood pressure varies between people with different diabetes outcomes, such as controlled or uncontrolled diabetes. It helps us understand the connection between blood pressure and diabetes management. Key Insight: Elevated blood pressure (above 130/80 mmHg) is common among people with poorly controlled diabetes and can lead to further complications like heart disease and kidney problems.

The heatmap can help identify patterns that suggest a higher risk for developing diabetes, such as high glucose levels correlating strongly with high BMI and age.

Liver Insights

Age Distribution (Liver)

This chart shows how liver health varies across different age groups. It helps us understand if certain liver conditions are more common in specific age ranges. Key Insight: Liver diseases, such as fatty liver disease and cirrhosis, become more common in older adults, particularly those over 50. However, the rise of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) is noticeable among younger adults due to obesity and poor dietary habits.

{{ graph_liver_age | safe }}

Total Bilirubin vs Alkaline Phosphatase

This graph shows the relationship between two liver enzymes: total bilirubin and alkaline phosphatase. These enzymes are important for liver function, and this chart helps us see if there's a connection between them. Key Insight: Elevated bilirubin levels can indicate liver dysfunction or bile duct obstruction, while elevated alkaline phosphatase levels are often associated with liver damage or disease. A balance in these levels is crucial for healthy liver function.

{{ graph_bilirubin_alp | safe }}

A/G Ratio Distribution (Liver)

This chart shows the distribution of the Albumin-to-Globulin (A/G) ratio in people with liver conditions. This ratio can help doctors assess liver health and function. Key Insight: A normal A/G ratio typically ranges between 1.1 and 2.5. A lower ratio (below 1.1) may indicate liver disease, malnutrition, or kidney conditions, while a higher ratio can be associated with certain types of infections or autoimmune disorders.
